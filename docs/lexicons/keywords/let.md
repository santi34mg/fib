# `let`

`let` is used for mutable and immutable variable declaration.

There are multiple syntaxes for variable declaration.

1. Variable declarations may or may not use the `mut` keyword to declare the binding as mutable.
2. Variable declarations may or may not specify a type.
3. Variable declarations may or may not specify an initalization expression.

**Syntax**:

```
let <identifier>
let <identifier> <type>
let <identifier> <type> = <expression>
let <identifier> = <expression>
let mut <identifier>
let mut <identifier> <type>
let mut <identifier> <type> = <expression>
let mut <identifier> = <expression>
```
